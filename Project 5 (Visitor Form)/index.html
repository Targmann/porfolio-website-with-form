About

Jacob Steimle is a neo-orchestral composer and producer based in Utah, originally from San Diego, California. Raised in a musical household, he began piano lessons at a young age under the guidance of his mother, a self-taught pianist. Over time, his curiosity led him to teach himself a wide variety of additional instruments, including ukulele, Irish whistle, trumpet, and mandolin.

Immersed in classical music throughout his childhood, Jacob developed a particular love for cinematic soundtracks, drawn to their ability to tell stories without words. As a pre-teen, he began composing simple piano pieces using notation software, gradually expanding into MIDI orchestration. What started as experimentation evolved into a deep commitment to the craft.

After years of practice, he transitioned to a full digital audio workstation environment, refining his skills in composition, recording, mixing, mastering, and production. At 18, he completed his first full-length album, Philharmonia, marking a major milestone in his artistic journey.

He releases music under the name The Universal Language, a project built on the belief that music transcends culture, background, and borders. Through his work, Jacob aims to create sound that connects, communicates, and resonates universally. No matter where someone is or where they come from, music can meet them there.
